1762

Caloocan, formerly known as Aromahan or Libis Espina, became a part of Tondo.

1815

Caloocan separated from Tondo, establishing itself as a distinct municipality. This was a crucial step in its development as an independent town.

1899

The Battle of Caloocan was one of the opening engagements of the Philippineâ€“American War, and was fought between an American force under the command of Arthur MacArthur Jr. and Filipino defenders led by Antonio Luna in February 1899.

1901

Caloocan became part of the historical province of Rizal.

1961

Republic Act No. 3278, signed by President Carlos P. Garcia, granted Caloocan cityhood.

1962

Caloocan was officially inducted as a city on February 16th, a date still commemorated as its founding anniversary.

1975

Caloocan became part of the National Capital Region (Metro Manila) through Presidential Decree No. 824.

1986

Following the People Power Revolution, Caloocan underwent political changes with the appointment of new local officials.

2000s-Present

Caloocan has seen continued urban development, including the expansion of commercial centers, residential areas, and infrastructure projects, solidifying its role as a key component of Metro Manila.
