Bonifacio Monument

A national landmark designed by Guillermo Tolentino, honoring Andres Bonifacio and the Katipunan.

La Loma Cemetery

One of the oldest cemeteries in Metro Manila, with Spanish colonial history. La Loma Cemetery in Caloocan is one of the oldest cemeteries in the Philippines, with a chapel that served as a fortress for Filipino soldiers during the Filipino-American War.

San Roque Cathedral

The seat of the Diocese of Kalookan, a place of faith and heritage. San Roque Cathedral Parish, commonly known as Caloocan Cathedral.

Caloocan City Hall

The center of government and administration, serving the people of Caloocan.

University of Caloocan City - South (UCC)

The University of Caloocan City is a public-type local university established in 1971 and formerly called Caloocan City Community College and Caloocan City Polytechnic College.

Manila Central University

Manila Central University, formerly known as the Escuela de Farmacia del Liceo de Manila, is a private, non-sectarian, stock basic and higher education institution located on EDSA, Caloocan, Philippines. It was founded in 1904 by Alejandro M. Albert, who also was its first director.
