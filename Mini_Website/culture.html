Pamaypay Festival

The Pamaypay ng Caloocan Festival is a significant festival in Caloocan, celebrating the city's founding anniversary and highlighting the traditional hand-held fan (pamaypay) with street-dancing competitions.

Traditional Dances

The city preserves traditional Filipino dances such as Tinikling and Carinosa, performed in schools and cultural gatherings. These dances reflect Filipino artistry, grace, and values of unity. Dance culture in Caloocan City is a mix of traditional folk dances, competitive performances, and modern dance genres.

Food & Cuisine

The city offers classic Filipino dishes like adobo and halo-halo at traditional restaurants and eateries. Boy Ching Woo, a historic Chinese restaurant established in 1939, is known for its buttered chicken and lechon con tokwa.

Sto. Nino De Pajotan Festival (A traditional Parade)

This religious festival features street dancing and processions to honor the Child Jesus, showcasing the city's deep faith and community spirit.

Tai To Hien Tuah Taoist Temple

This temple is a place of worship and spiritual reflection that showcases the blend of Chinese and Filipino culture in the city.
